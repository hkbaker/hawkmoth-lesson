Introduction

The Hummingbird Hawk-Moth (Macroglossum stellatarum) is frequently mistaken for a hummingbird because of its hovering flight and rapid wingbeats. This microlearning module emphasizes its distinctive behaviors and adaptations. You will learn to identify a hummingbird hawkmoth and distinguish it from hummingbirds. Imagine walking through a garden at dusk and seeing something darting between flowers. At first, it appears to be a hummingbird— but is it? Use the clues provided here to help identify the tiny creature.

Fun Facts

  • Feeds on nectar while hovering like a hummingbird.
  • Has a long proboscis to reach deep flowers.
  • Active during the day, unlike most moths.
  • Wings beat up to 85 times per second.
